Why read books when we forget?

Reading is one of my favorite past times. There was a point in time where I took the one book a week challenge where I tried to finish a non-fiction book every week. The idea that successful people read a lot was something I wanted to emulate. However, a few months in, I gave up. I lost motivation, not because I couldn’t finish a book once a week. It’s because I forget most of what I read very quickly.

Yet, learning seems to be a frustrating and futile experience. Why frustrating and futile you ask? Because I tend to forget most of what I learned.
